Well I guess this is my fault for complaining about not having much to do. Juniper managed to get herself a nasty case of appendicitis. Fortunately, this is something that I could treat with my eyes closed. Not that it matters. So can that magic medbay cylinder. I would contest whether or not it does as good a job as myself, but what can I do besides follow protocol?
\n
\nAnyway, Juniper is going to be fine. She might find herself disappointed when she realises how many days off she’s actually going to get. It really doesn’t take long to recover from appendicitis with equipment like this. More than enough time to get her listening to some of ol’ Vermillion’s classic tunes though. 
\n
\n------
\n
\nI think that after only a single day stuck in bed, Juniper is beginning to wish that she wasn’t. She’s already complaining about my music, so she’s starting to get back to her normal self at least. I think it’s helping that she is accompanied by four kittens. She’ll be up and about tomorrow, in time for the probe launch on Monday. 
\n
\nI’m not saying this was all a ruse to miss the probe launch, but if it was, I’ve thwarted her plans.   
\n
\nJuniper wasn’t too happy with that last line. I’m reading this log aloud as a form of entertainment.
\n
\n------
\n
\nThe probe launch went poorly. I really don’t have much more insight into the matter. Honestly, I was watching the launch on the same screens as everyone else and I didn’t understand a thing that was going on. Still, I feel down about it all the same. 
\n
\nWe’re becoming a little family here, with a common goal. The success of this mission has very little impact on me, but it could make these guys’ careers, so it’s important to me that this all goes well for them. We’ll get back on track, I have no doubts about that.
